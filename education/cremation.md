How we treat our dead tells us a great deal about ourselves and our society. As the saying goes: we burn trash, but we bury treasure.

Jewish tradition regards the body as sacred — a vessel that housed the soul and carried out mitzvot throughout a person's life. For that reason, Jewish law calls for the body's natural return to the earth through in-ground burial, and does not permit cremation. The pages below offer several perspectives on why.

Jewish end-of-life traditions and laws touch on many of the beliefs that form the foundation of Judaism: the neshamah (soul), the afterlife, reward and punishment, and resurrection (t'chiyas hameisim). By studying these practices, we gain a deeper understanding of these beliefs, along with an appreciation for the profundity of halachah and our traditions.

## Recommended reading

One of the most accessible explorations of these ideas is the essay **"Immortality and the Soul" by Rabbi Aryeh Kaplan**, which draws on the teachings of the Talmud, Midrash, and Kabbalists to describe the soul's existence beyond the body. We encourage you to read it in full at its original source.

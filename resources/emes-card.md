The NASCK EMES Program and the EMES card address a range of concerns that every Jewish adult should consider:

- At the time of an emergency, how will anyone know whether a medical or end-of-life directive was signed?
- Changing laws and varied family structures make it essential for every Jewish adult to designate a health-care proxy.
- The complexity of medical and end-of-life decisions calls for halachic guidance.
- Many states do not accommodate a family's religious objection to autopsy — but a declared objection by the individual carries legal weight.
- If someone wished for a traditional burial, how will anyone know?
- New laws give a designated agent the primary right to make post-mortem decisions.
- With more Jews choosing cremation, it is more important than ever to make traditional burial wishes clear.

The EMES wallet card, carried with your ID, signals your wishes and points first responders and family to your designated proxy and rabbi.

## Get your EMES card

The EMES card is provided together with the Halachic Living Will. Visit the [Halachic / Living Wills]({{ '/resources/halachic-living-wills/' | relative_url }}) page to download the directive and request your free wallet card.
